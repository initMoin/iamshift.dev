toDō

where structure meets intention

toDō is a task system built around clarity, momentum, and intent. It is designed to help people move from vague mental clutter to deliberate action without the noise, bloat, or friction that turns most productivity tools into chores of their own.

The app treats a task as more than a checkbox. A toDō can carry timing, recurrence, notes, tags, and supporting structure, while still feeling light, fast, and native to the Apple ecosystem.

This project is also a statement of how I build: calm interfaces, precise behavior, thoughtful hierarchy, and product decisions that respect the user’s attention instead of competing for it.

showcase

features

Capture one focus at a time.

toDō is built around a simple principle: one clear focus is stronger than a noisy pile of half-decisions. The app is meant to help users get a task out of their head, give it structure, and move.

Instead of asking people to manage a system before they manage their work, toDō lowers the friction. Capture comes first. Structure follows. Momentum stays intact.

This is where the app starts to differ. It does not try to impress users with complexity. It tries to help them act.
